APJ Abdul Kalam

A.P.J. Abdul Kalam, known as the “Missile Man of India,” was an esteemed aerospace scientist and the 11th President of India, serving from 2002 to 2007. Born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, he played a pivotal role in advancing India’s missile and nuclear weapons programs. His significant contributions include working with the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) and the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO), where he was instrumental in the Polar Satellite Launch Vehicle (PSLV) project. Dr. Kalam’s leadership in the 1998 Pokhran-II nuclear tests affirmed India’s position as a nuclear power. Beyond his scientific prowess, he was a visionary who promoted plans like Technology Vision 2020, aiming to transform India into a developed nation. His inspirational life and works, including his famous autobiography “Wings of Fire,” continue to influence and motivate millions around the world.

  • Dr. Kalam was awarded the Padma Bhushan in 1981, the Padma Vibhushan in 1990, and the Bharat Ratna in 1997.
  • He received honorary doctorates from 48 universities worldwide.
  • Dr. Kalam was a visiting professor at several prestigious institutions, including the Indian Institute of Management Ahmedabad and the Indian Institute of Management Indore.
  • He was known for his humility, dedication to education, and love for children, often interacting with students to share his knowledge and experiences.
